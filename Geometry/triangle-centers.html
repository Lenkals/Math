Acute Triangle
G — Centroid
Center of Mass
The point where all three medians intersect. A median connects a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side.
Divides each median in a 2∶1 ratio from vertex. This is where the triangle would balance perfectly on a pin.
O — Circumcenter
Circumscribed Circle Center
Where the three perpendicular bisectors of the sides meet. Equidistant from all three vertices.
For acute triangles it lies inside; for right triangles on the hypotenuse midpoint; for obtuse triangles outside.
H — Orthocenter
Altitude Intersection
The point where all three altitudes meet. An altitude is the perpendicular from a vertex to the opposite side.
G, O and H are always collinear on the Euler Line, with HG = 2·GO always holding true.
I — Incenter
Inscribed Circle Center
Where the three angle bisectors meet. Each bisector divides an interior angle into two equal parts.
Always lies inside the triangle. The incircle is the largest circle that fits entirely within the triangle.
