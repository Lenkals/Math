01 — Power Rule
Power Rule
xⁿ
The fundamental rule of polynomial differentiation
f(x) =xⁿ
f′(x) =n · xn−1
f″(x) =n(n−1) · xn−2
Bring the exponent n down as a coefficient, then reduce the power by 1.
Works for any real n — including fractions (√x = x^½) and negatives (1/x = x⁻¹).
Exponent n = 3

02 — Exponential
Exponential
eˣ
The only function that is its own derivative
f(x) =eˣ
f′(x) =eˣ
f″(x) =eˣ
f(x) =aˣ
f′(x) =aˣ · ln(a)
eˣ is self-replicating — differentiating it gives back exactly eˣ.
For general base a, a factor of ln(a) appears because eˣ is the "natural" base.
Base a = e
f(x)=aˣ
f′(x)=aˣln(a)

03 — Logarithmic
Logarithmic
ln(x)
The inverse of the exponential function
f(x) =ln(x)
f′(x) =1 / x
f″(x) =−1 / x²
f(x) =logₐ(x)
f′(x) =1 / (x · ln a)
The slope of ln(x) at any point is exactly 1/x. At x=1 slope=1, at x=2 slope=½, at x=10 slope=0.1 — the curve gets flatter as x grows.
Base a = e
f(x)=logₐ(x)
f′(x)=1/(x·ln a)

04 — Trigonometric
Trigonometric
sin · cos · tan
Cyclic derivatives — they loop every 4 steps
d/dxsin(x) = cos(x)
d/dxcos(x) = −sin(x)
d/dxtan(x) = sec²(x)
Differentiate sin 4 times: sin → cos → −sin → −cos → sin again.
The derivative is always a phase shift of π/2 (90°).

05 — Inverse Trigonometric
Inverse Trig
arcsin · arccos · arctan
Algebraic derivatives from transcendental inverses
d/dxarcsin(x) = 1/√(1−x²)
d/dxarccos(x) = −1/√(1−x²)
d/dxarctan(x) = 1/(1+x²)
arctan's derivative 1/(1+x²) is defined everywhere — no domain issues.
arcsin/arccos derivatives have √(1−x²) in denominator, so domain is (−1, 1).

06 — Hyperbolic
Hyperbolic
sinh · cosh · tanh
Exponential analogues of the trig functions
d/dxsinh(x) = cosh(x)
d/dxcosh(x) = sinh(x)
d/dxtanh(x) = sech²(x)
Unlike trig, cosh differentiates to sinh (not −sinh). No sign flip!
sinh(x) = (eˣ − e⁻ˣ)/2, cosh(x) = (eˣ + e⁻ˣ)/2 — both defined by exponentials.
